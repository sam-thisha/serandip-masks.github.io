What are Sri Lankan traditional masks?

Sri Lankan traditional masks are wooden masks used in traditional Sri Lankan dances, rituals, and ceremonies. They are hand-carved and painted in vibrant colors and designs, each representing a different character or deity from Sri Lankan folklore.

What are the masks made of?

Sri Lankan traditional masks are made of lightweight, durable wood such as ebony, mahogany, or halmilla. They are then painted with natural dyes and pigments.

How are the masks made?

The masks are hand-carved by skilled artisans using traditional tools and techniques. The process involves selecting the wood, shaping it with chisels and knives, and then painting it with natural dyes and pigments.

What is the significance of the masks?

The masks are an important part of Sri Lankan culture and tradition. They are used in traditional dances, rituals, and ceremonies to represent different characters and deities. They are believed to have the power to ward off evil spirits and bring good luck.

How do I know if the masks are authentic?

Our masks are made by skilled artisans using traditional techniques and materials. Each mask comes with a certificate of authenticity and a description of the character or deity it represents.

How do I care for my mask?

To keep your mask in good condition, avoid exposing it to direct sunlight or moisture. Use a soft cloth to gently clean the surface and avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive materials.
